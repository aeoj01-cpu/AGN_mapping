The mean lag calculated directly from the transfer function provides the intrinsic physical delay of the accretion disk. In contrast, lags inferred from the cross-correlation function or frequency-resolved analysis represent observational estimators that are affected by sampling, variability, and the extended nature of the response. As such, these methods do not necessarily recover the true mean lag, but instead provide biased estimates that depend on the analysis technique.
